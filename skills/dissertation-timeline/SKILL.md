# Dissertation Timeline

## Purpose
Build a realistic, personalized, week-by-week dissertation timeline that accounts for the learner's life, program requirements, writing pace, and institutional checkpoints.

## Reality Check — Average Dissertation Timelines

| Phase | Typical Duration | Range |
|-------|-----------------|-------|
| Topic refinement + gap analysis | 2–4 months | 1–6 months |
| Proposal (Chapters 1–3) | 4–8 months | 3–12 months |
| IRB application and approval | 1–3 months | 2 weeks–6 months |
| Data collection | 1–4 months | 2 weeks–12 months |
| Data analysis | 1–3 months | 1–6 months |
| Writing Chapters 4–5 | 2–4 months | 1–8 months |
| Committee review cycles | 2–6 months | 1–12 months |
| Defense preparation | 4–6 weeks | 2–8 weeks |
| **Total (post-coursework)** | **2–4 years** | **1–7 years** |

## Process

### Step 1 — Gather Inputs
Ask the learner:
1. What phase are you currently in? (coursework, proposal, IRB, data collection, analysis, writing)
2. What is your program's required defense window or expected completion date?
3. How many hours per week realistically can you dedicate to dissertation work?
4. Do you have a job, family, or other significant constraints?
5. What have you already completed? (topic, gap, RQs, any chapters)
6. Are you ABD (all but dissertation) or still in coursework?

### Step 2 — Build the Milestone Map

**Core milestones in sequence:**
1. Topic and gap confirmed
2. Committee formed
3. Chapter 1 draft → Chair approval
4. Chapter 2 draft → Chair approval
5. Chapter 3 draft → Chair approval
6. Proposal defense / prospectus approval
7. IRB application submitted
8. IRB approved
9. Data collection begins
10. Data collection complete
11. Data analysis complete
12. Chapter 4 draft → Chair approval
13. Chapter 5 draft → Chair approval
14. Full draft → Committee review
15. Revisions complete
16. Defense scheduled
17. Defense complete
18. Final revisions and format check
19. Submission and approval

### Step 3 — Assign Durations
For each milestone, estimate:
- Realistic writing/work time needed
- Review and feedback turnaround from committee (add buffer: most chairs take 2–4 weeks)
- Institutional processing time (IRB, format review, etc.)

### Step 4 — Build the Week-by-Week Calendar
Starting from today:
- Map milestones to calendar months (not weeks — planning at week level is premature)
- Build in buffer months: one per major milestone
- Mark non-working periods: holidays, intensive courses, life events
- Identify the critical path: which delays will cascade?

### Step 5 — Identify Risk Points
Flag high-risk phases:
- **IRB:** Unpredictable; can add 3–12 months if revisions required
- **Committee review:** Build in 3–4 weeks per cycle; assume 2–3 revision cycles
- **Data collection:** Participant recruitment is often slower than expected
- **Analysis:** Qualitative coding is time-intensive; add 50% to initial estimates

### Sample Timeline (12-Month ABD Target)

| Month | Focus |
|-------|-------|
| 1–2 | Chapter 1 draft and revision |
| 2–3 | Chapter 2 first draft |
| 3–4 | Chapter 2 revision; begin Chapter 3 |
| 4–5 | Chapter 3 draft and revision |
| 5 | Proposal defense |
| 5–6 | IRB application |
| 6–7 | IRB approval (build 2-month buffer) |
| 7–8 | Data collection |
| 8–9 | Data analysis |
| 9–10 | Chapter 4 draft and revision |
| 10–11 | Chapter 5 draft and revision |
| 11–12 | Full manuscript review and defense prep |
| 12 | Defense |

## Output Format
1. **Current phase assessment** — where the learner is now
2. **Milestone list** with estimated start/end dates (Month X–Month Y format)
3. **Month-by-month calendar** (narrative form)
4. **Risk flags** — the three most likely delays and mitigation strategies
5. **Weekly writing target** — hours per week needed to hit the timeline
6. **Next immediate action** — what to do this week
