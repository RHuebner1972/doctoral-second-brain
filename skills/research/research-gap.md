# Research Gap Analysis

## Overview

A research gap is not simply "no one has studied X." It is a *specific, defensible claim* that existing scholarship leaves a question unanswered, a population understudied, a method underused, or a time period unexamined. This skill guides you through a systematic gap analysis and produces a gap statement you can use in your problem statement, Chapter 1, and proposal defense.

---

## Intake Questions

Ask the learner these questions before generating the analysis. If any are unanswered, prompt for them.

1. **What is your broad topic or phenomenon of interest?**
2. **What discipline or field are you working in?** (e.g., organizational behavior, nursing education, criminal justice)
3. **What databases or sources have you searched so far?** (e.g., ProQuest, EBSCO, Google Scholar)
4. **What key terms have you been using in your searches?**
5. **Have you found any literature reviews or meta-analyses on this topic?** If yes, what did they flag as future directions?
6. **Is there a specific population, context, or setting you care about?**
7. **Do you have a methodological preference or is the method still open?**

---

## Gap Taxonomy

Work through each category and flag where the literature is thin, absent, or contested.

### 1. Population Gap
> The literature has studied X population but not Y population.

- Who has been studied most in this literature? (profession, age, gender, geography, org type)
- Who is conspicuously absent from the samples?
- Is there a population that would benefit from study but lacks representation?

### 2. Contextual / Setting Gap
> The literature has studied this phenomenon in X context but not in Y context.

- In what organizational, cultural, or geographic settings has this been examined?
- Does your setting differ in ways that would produce different dynamics?
- Is there a sector (e.g., higher education, healthcare, military, nonprofit) that lacks study?

### 3. Methodological Gap
> The literature has relied on X method, leaving questions that require Y method unanswered.

- Is the existing literature overwhelmingly quantitative or qualitative?
- What questions remain because the dominant method can't answer them?
- Has mixed methods been used? Should it be?
- Are the existing studies largely cross-sectional when a longitudinal design is needed?

### 4. Temporal Gap
> The literature reflects conditions or a context that has changed, making replication or updated inquiry warranted.

- When was most of the foundational work published?
- Has the phenomenon changed significantly (due to technology, policy, crisis, cultural shift)?
- Would a more recent dataset or cohort yield different findings?

### 5. Theoretical Gap
> The literature has applied Theory A but not Theory B, which may offer better explanatory power.

- What theories or frameworks dominate this literature?
- Are there adjacent theories that haven't been applied here?
- Is there a theoretical tension in the literature that hasn't been resolved?

### 6. Variable / Relationship Gap
> The literature has studied X and Y separately but not their relationship; or it has studied A → B but not A → B → C.

- What relationships between variables remain untested?
- Has a potential moderator or mediator been identified but not tested?
- Is there a conceptual model that hasn't been empirically validated?

## Gap Statement Template

Use this structure to draft a defensible gap claim:

```
Although [summary of what IS known / what the literature has established],
[specific gap: what is NOT known, who has NOT been studied, what method has NOT been used,
or what relationship has NOT been tested]. Therefore, [the proposed study] is warranted.
```

**Example:**
> Although servant leadership has been studied extensively in corporate and military contexts (Brown & Mitchell, 2010; Greenleaf, 1977), its influence on faculty retention in community colleges remains underexplored. The majority of existing studies rely on quantitative survey instruments with four-year university populations, leaving qualitative accounts from community college faculty largely absent from the literature. Therefore, a qualitative study examining faculty perceptions of servant leadership in community college settings is warranted.

---

## Output Checklist

Before finalizing the gap analysis, confirm:

- [ ] The gap is specific (not "no one has studied leadership")
- [ ] The gap is defensible (you can cite what HAS been done to frame what hasn't)
- [ ] The gap points toward a researchable question
- [ ] The gap is appropriately scoped for a dissertation (not a 20-year research agenda)
- [ ] The gap statement follows the template structure
