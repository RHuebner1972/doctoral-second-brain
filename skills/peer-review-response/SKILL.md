# Peer Review Response

## Purpose
Help the learner craft professional, scholarly, point-by-point responses to committee, chair, or peer reviewer feedback — turning critique into a structured revision dialogue.

## The Core Principle
A good review response does three things:
1. **Acknowledges** the reviewer's concern without being defensive
2. **Explains** what was changed and where
3. **Shows** the revised text or data (or justifies why no change was made)

Never argue with reviewers. Even when you disagree, the tone must remain collegial and respectful.

## Response Document Structure

### Cover Letter (Dissertation Revisions)
> Dear [Committee Chair / Reviewer Name],
>
> Thank you for your thorough review of [dissertation title / manuscript title]. I have carefully considered each comment and have prepared a detailed response below. Revisions are organized by reviewer comment, with a description of the change made and the revised text where applicable.
>
> I believe these revisions have strengthened the manuscript considerably. I look forward to your continued guidance.
>
> Respectfully,
> [Name]

### Point-by-Point Response Template

For each comment:

**Reviewer Comment [#]:**
> [Paste exact comment here]

**Author Response:**
> [Acknowledge the point, explain the revision, and cite location]

**Revised Text:**
> [Show the new or revised passage, or state "No change was made because..."]

## Response Strategies by Comment Type

### Requests for Clarification
> "The reviewer correctly identifies that [concept] was not sufficiently defined in the original text. To address this, I have added a definition of [concept] on page X: [quote revised text]."

### Requests for Additional Literature
> "I appreciate this suggestion. I have incorporated [Author, year] into the literature review in Section X.X (page X), as it directly addresses [topic]. The revised passage reads: [quote]."

### Methodological Challenges
> "This is an important concern. I have added a discussion of [issue] in Section 3.X under [heading], acknowledging this as a limitation of the chosen design. The addition reads: [quote]."

### Requests You Disagree With (Diplomatic Pushback)
> "I appreciate the reviewer's perspective on this point. After careful consideration, I have respectfully maintained the original approach because [scholarly rationale with citation]. However, I have added language on page X to acknowledge this alternative view: [quote]."
*Use this sparingly. Only push back when you have a strong scholarly argument.*

### Requests for Data or Evidence
> "The reviewer is correct that [claim] required stronger support. I have added [citation] and [citation] in paragraph X to bolster this point. The revised text reads: [quote]."

## Process

### Step 1 — Gather Inputs
Ask the learner to:
1. Paste each reviewer comment (numbered)
2. Indicate the dissertation chapter and section where the comment applies
3. Indicate whether they agree or disagree with each comment
4. Share what revisions they've made (or plan to make)

### Step 2 — Categorize Each Comment
- Clarification requested
- Additional literature needed
- Methodological concern
- Framing or writing quality
- Factual correction
- Disagree / pushback needed

### Step 3 — Draft Responses
Use the templates above, customizing language for each comment.

### Step 4 — Review for Tone
Every response must be:
- [ ] Respectful and collegial
- [ ] Specific (not vague)
- [ ] Traceable (page numbers, section names)
- [ ] Scholarly (no defensive language)

## Output Format
1. **Cover letter** (brief, professional)
2. **Numbered point-by-point responses** (one block per comment)
3. **Revision tracker** (table: Comment # | Type | Change Made | Location)
4. **Unresolved items** (comments the learner still needs to address)
