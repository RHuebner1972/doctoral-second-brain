# Deep Focus Protocol

## Purpose
Guide the learner through a structured pre-session ritual that builds the conditions for deep, uninterrupted doctoral work — moving from distracted to focused in under 10 minutes.

## The Science Behind Deep Work
Cal Newport's research distinguishes between shallow work (email, logistics, low-stakes tasks) and deep work (cognitively demanding, high-value output). Dissertation writing is unambiguously deep work. It requires:
- Extended uninterrupted time (90+ minutes is ideal)
- A single cognitive task
- Protection from interruption
- A clear entry point

Most doctoral learners try to write while remaining accessible. This is the primary focus killer.

## The Pre-Session Protocol (8–10 Minutes)

### Step 1 — Environment Check (2 min)
Physical environment matters. Configure:

- [ ] **Surface clear** — only dissertation-related materials visible
- [ ] **Second screen** (if available) set to the dissertation document only
- [ ] **Phone** face-down, on silent, or in another room
- [ ] **Browser** — close all tabs except sources needed for this session
- [ ] **Notifications** — email, Slack, Teams all silenced or closed
- [ ] **Beverage/snack** — get it now, before the timer starts
- [ ] **Temperature** — slightly cool is better for focus than warm
- [ ] **Sound** — choose one: silence, white noise, brown noise, or lyric-free music (binaural beats, classical, lo-fi)

### Step 2 — Intention Setting (2 min)
Answer these three questions in writing (or out loud):

1. **What am I working on?**
> "I am working on the data analysis subsection of Chapter 3."

2. **What is the one deliverable I will produce in this session?**
> "A complete rough draft of the trustworthiness section — approximately 400 words."

3. **What is the first sentence I will write?**
> Write it down. This is the entry point. The moment the session starts, type this sentence.

### Step 3 — Constraints (2 min)
Name your constraints so they don't become decisions mid-session:

- **Time boundary:** "This session ends at ___."
- **Scope boundary:** "I am writing ONLY this section. If I think of something else, I will write it in a parking lot and come back."
- **Research boundary:** "If I need a citation I don't have, I will write [CITATION NEEDED] and keep moving."
- **Editing boundary:** "I am not editing this session. I am only drafting."

### Step 4 — Commitment Device (1 min)
A commitment device is a self-imposed constraint that makes distraction harder:

Choose one:
- **Physical:** Turn off WiFi for the session duration
- **App:** Use Freedom, Cold Turkey, or Focus Mode to block sites
- **Social:** Tell someone "I am writing until [time]; please don't disturb me"
- **Ritualistic:** A specific drink, location, or piece of music that signals "we are in deep work now"
- **Written:** Write "I commit to [deliverable] by [time]" and put it where you can see it

### Step 5 — Begin (1 min)
Set the timer. Type the first sentence you wrote in Step 2.

That's it. You're in.

## Managing Intrusive Thoughts During the Session

The mind will offer interruptions: "I should check that email," "I forgot to do X," "What if my methodology is wrong?"

**The Parking Lot:** Keep a small notepad beside you. When an intrusive thought arrives, write it in the parking lot in one word or phrase — then return immediately to the draft. The parking lot captures it; your brain can stop holding it.

Do not act on parking lot items until the session is complete.

---

## The Transition Out (5 min)

When the timer ends:
1. **Do not stop mid-sentence** if you're in flow — finish the thought
2. **Write the first sentence of the next session** — this is the single highest-ROI action you can take
3. **Log the session:** Words written, time, section, how focused you felt (1–5)
4. **Reopen the world gradually** — notifications before email; email before social

## Focus Environment Profiles

**Home office:**
White noise generator, standing desk if possible, dedicated "work only" chair or spot

**Café:**
Noise-canceling headphones, back-to-wall seating, purchased something so you're not anxious about the seat

**Library:**
Ideal environment — use it when you need a focus emergency

**Commute writing:**
Phone or tablet with offline access, voice dictation for rough drafts, keyboard case if possible

## Output Format
1. **Session intention** (filled in by the learner)
2. **Environment checklist** (to run through before starting)
3. **Constraints named** (time, scope, research, editing)
4. **Commitment device chosen**
5. **First sentence** (the entry point to begin writing)
6. **Parking lot** (blank — ready to capture intrusive thoughts)
