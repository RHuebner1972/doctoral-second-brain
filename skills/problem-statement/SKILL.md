# Problem Statement

## Purpose
Produce a scholarly, evidence-grounded problem statement that establishes *why* this study must exist — the "so what" that justifies the entire dissertation.

## What a Problem Statement Is NOT
- It is not the research gap alone (the gap is the *what*; the problem is the *why it matters*)
- It is not a solution or a research question
- It is not a personal anecdote without scholarly support
- It is not a problem with the existing research — it is a problem in the world or practice

## Components of a Strong Problem Statement

1. **The Broader Context** — What is the field, phenomenon, or domain? (2–3 sentences grounded in recent literature)
2. **The Known** — What do we already know? What has been established? (cite 3–5 sources)
3. **The Unknown / Gap** — What is missing from the knowledge base? (link to gap analysis)
4. **The Consequence** — What happens because this gap exists? What is the cost — to practitioners, organizations, individuals, policy, or theory?
5. **The Call to Action** — A statement that this study is needed (do not state the study here — that comes in the purpose statement)

## Process

### Step 1 — Gather Inputs
Ask the learner:
1. What is the gap you've identified?
2. Who is harmed or disadvantaged by this gap?
3. What evidence exists that this is a real-world problem (not just a literature gap)?
4. What is the broader context — organizational, social, policy, or theoretical?

### Step 2 — Draft Using the ABCDE Structure

**A — Anchor** (broader domain and significance)
> "In [field/domain], [phenomenon] has been recognized as [significance]..."

**B — Body of knowledge** (what is known)
> "Research has established that [X, Y, Z] (Author, year; Author, year)..."

**C — Crack** (the gap)
> "However, [specific population/context/method] remains underexplored..."

**D — Damage** (consequence of the gap)
> "The absence of this knowledge leaves [stakeholders] without [guidance/evidence/frameworks], resulting in [consequence]..."

**E — End signal** (transition to purpose)
> "Addressing this gap requires [type of investigation]..."

### Step 3 — Strengthen with Evidence
- Every claim of a problem must be supported by a citation
- Use recent sources (within 5–7 years preferred, within 10 acceptable)
- Include practitioner sources (industry reports, policy documents) alongside scholarly sources where the problem is applied

### Step 4 — Review Criteria
Before finalizing, verify:
- [ ] Is the problem clearly stated (not implied)?
- [ ] Is the problem grounded in 3+ cited sources?
- [ ] Is the consequence of inaction explicit?
- [ ] Does the statement avoid proposing a solution?
- [ ] Is the scope appropriate (not too broad, not too narrow)?

## Output Format
Return:
1. **Draft problem statement** (200–400 words, APA 7th citations included as placeholders)
2. **Annotation** — which ABCDE component each paragraph serves
3. **Gaps in evidence** — what the learner still needs to find to strengthen it
4. **Next step** — purpose statement or research questions
