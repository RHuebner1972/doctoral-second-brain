# Dissertation Outline

## Purpose
Scaffold a complete, section-level dissertation outline tailored to the learner's study — methodology, topic, and degree type.

## The Five-Chapter Model (Standard)

Most dissertations follow a five-chapter structure. Below is the complete section-level outline with guidance for each.

---

### Chapter 1 — Introduction

| Section | Purpose | Target Length |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| Background of the Problem | Context and significance; why this topic matters | 1–2 pages |
| Problem Statement | The specific, evidence-based problem being studied | 1 page |
| Purpose Statement | The intent of the study (what you will do, not why) | 1 paragraph |
| Research Questions | Central and sub-questions (and hypotheses if quantitative) | ½ page |
| Theoretical/Conceptual Framework | Brief introduction to the anchoring theory | ½–1 page |
| Nature of the Study | Overview of methodology and design | ½ page |
| Definitions | Operational and conceptual definitions of key terms | 1–2 pages |
| Assumptions | What you assume to be true but cannot verify | ½ page |
| Scope and Delimitations | What the study includes and excludes | ½ page |
| Limitations | Weaknesses you acknowledge but cannot control | ½ page |
| Significance | Why it matters — to theory, practice, and policy | 1 page |
| Summary | Chapter recap and transition to Chapter 2 | ½ page |

---

### Chapter 2 — Literature Review

| Section | Purpose | Target Length |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| Introduction | Purpose, scope, and organization of the review | 1 page |
| Theoretical/Conceptual Framework | Full treatment of the anchoring theory | 3–5 pages |
| Theme 1 | First major concept/construct | 3–5 pages |
| Theme 2 | Second major concept/construct | 3–5 pages |
| Theme 3 | Third major concept/construct (add as needed) | 3–5 pages |
| Synthesis and Gap | What the literature establishes and leaves open | 2–3 pages |
| Summary | Chapter recap and transition | ½ page |

---

### Chapter 3 — Methodology

| Section | Purpose | Target Length |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| Introduction | Overview and purpose restatement | ½ page |
| Research Design and Rationale | Paradigm, approach, tradition — justified | 2–3 pages |
| Role of the Researcher | Positionality, reflexivity, bias mitigation | 1–2 pages |
| Participants | Selection criteria, recruitment, sample size justification | 2–3 pages |
| Data Collection | Instruments/protocols, procedures, timeline | 2–4 pages |
| Data Analysis | How data will be analyzed (coding, statistics, etc.) | 2–3 pages |
| Trustworthiness / Validity | Credibility, transferability, dependability, confirmability (qual) OR validity, reliability (quant) | 2–3 pages |
| Ethical Considerations | IRB, consent, confidentiality, data storage | 1–2 pages |
| Summary | Chapter recap and transition | ½ page |

---

### Chapter 4 — Findings / Results

| Section | Purpose | Target Length |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| Introduction | Restate purpose and RQs; overview of chapter | ½–1 page |
| Participant Profiles (qual) OR Descriptive Statistics (quant) | Describe the sample | 2–3 pages |
| Data Collection Procedures | What was done (brief) | ½–1 page |
| Findings by Research Question | Address each RQ in turn | Varies |
| → RQ1 Findings | Themes, categories, or statistical results | 3–5 pages per RQ |
| → RQ2 Findings | | |
| → RQ3 Findings | | |
| Disconfirming Evidence (qual) | Negative cases, rival explanations | 1 page |
| Summary | Chapter recap and transition | ½ page |

---

### Chapter 5 — Discussion, Conclusions, and Recommendations

| Section | Purpose | Target Length |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| Introduction | Overview and restatement of purpose | ½ page |
| Interpretation of Findings | What the findings mean (connected to literature) | 3–5 pages |
| Connections to Theoretical Framework | How findings affirm, extend, or challenge the framework | 2–3 pages |
| Implications for Practice | Actionable recommendations for practitioners | 2–3 pages |
| Implications for Theory | Contributions to the knowledge base | 1–2 pages |
| Recommendations for Future Research | What should be studied next | 1–2 pages |
| Limitations | Reiterate study limitations with reflection | 1 page |
| Conclusions | Final synthesis — what does this all mean? | 1–2 pages |

## Process

### Step 1 — Gather Inputs
Ask the learner:
1. What is your methodology (qualitative, quantitative, mixed)?
2. What is your specific design (phenomenology, case study, survey, etc.)?
3. How many research questions do you have?
4. What degree and institution requirements apply?

### Step 2 — Customize the Outline
Adjust sections based on:
- **Qualitative:** Emphasize trustworthiness, positionality, rich participant description
- **Quantitative:** Add instrumentation detail, statistical analysis plan, power analysis
- **Mixed:** Add integration/meta-inference sections in Chapters 3, 4, and 5

### Step 3 — Estimate Length and Timeline
Standard dissertation targets:
- Chapter 1: 15–25 pages
- Chapter 2: 40–70 pages
- Chapter 3: 25–40 pages
- Chapter 4: 30–50 pages
- Chapter 5: 25–40 pages
- **Total:** 135–225 pages (excluding references and appendices)

## Output Format
1. **Customized chapter outline** (sections named, purpose annotated)
2. **Page length estimates** per chapter and section
3. **Writing order recommendation** (not always Chapter 1 first)
4. **Next step** — which chapter or section to start with and why
