# IRB & Research Ethics

## Purpose
Guide the learner through institutional review board (IRB) requirements, ethical design principles, and compliance with research regulations — ensuring human subjects are protected and the study meets institutional standards.

## Key Concept: What Is IRB?

**IRB = Institutional Review Board**
- Regulatory body that reviews research involving human subjects
- Ensures compliance with federal regulations (45 CFR 46, The Common Rule)
- Protects research participants' rights, safety, and welfare
- Required by U.S. institutions receiving federal funding
- Most universities require IRB approval regardless of funding source

### Process

#### Step 1 — Determine If You Need IRB Approval

Ask yourself:
1. Does my study involve human subjects? (Yes → likely needs IRB)
2. Do I collect data directly from people or their identifiable information? (Yes → likely needs IRB)
3. Is my research non-exempt? (Most dissertations are non-exempt → needs full review)

**Common Exempt Categories** (may not need approval):
- Archival research using de-identified/public documents
- Surveys of low-risk topics with no identifiable information
- Certain educational research practices
- **Check with your IRB; do not self-determine exemption**

#### Step 2 — Understand Your Ethical Obligations

**The Belmont Report (3 Principles)**

| Principle | What It Means | Your Responsibility |
|-----------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Respect for Persons** | Individuals are autonomous agents; protect those with diminished autonomy | Obtain informed consent; protect vulnerable populations |
| **Beneficence** | Maximize benefits; minimize harm | Design research that serves a meaningful purpose; mitigate risks |
| **Justice** | Fair distribution of research benefits & burdens | Don't over-burden vulnerable groups; ensure equitable participant selection |

**Core Ethical Considerations**
- **Informed Consent**: Participants understand what they're agreeing to
- **Minimization of Risk**: Research procedures don't expose people to unnecessary harm
- **Confidentiality & Privacy**: Protect participant identities and data
- **Data Security**: Secure storage, access restrictions, secure deletion protocols
- **Researcher Integrity**: No deception (except where ethically justified & justified to IRB)

#### Step 3 — Draft Your IRB Protocol

**Typical IRB Application Sections**

1. **Research Overview**
   - Study title
   - Purpose & significance
   - Specific aims/hypotheses
   - Duration of study

2. **Study Design & Procedures**
   - Research methodology (qualitative, quantitative, mixed)
   - Participant recruitment (how many, where, who)
   - Data collection methods (interviews, surveys, observations, etc.)
   - Timeline
   - **Clearly describe what participants will do step-by-step**

3. **Participant Population**
   - Number of participants
   - Age range
   - Inclusion/exclusion criteria
   - Any vulnerable populations (minors, pregnant women, prisoners, cognitively impaired, etc.)

4. **Informed Consent Form (ICF)**
   - Study purpose & procedures
   - Time commitment
   - Risks & discomforts
   - Benefits (if any)
   - Confidentiality & data protection
   - Right to withdraw without penalty
   - Researcher contact info & IRB contact info
   - **Must be written at 6th-8th grade reading level**

5. **Risk Assessment**
   - Identify all risks (physical, psychological, social, financial, legal)
   - For each risk, explain how it will be minimized
   - Assess overall risk as minimal or greater than minimal
   - **Example**: "Risk of emotional discomfort during interviews about trauma; mitigation: offer breaks, provide counseling referrals"

6. **Confidentiality & Data Protection**
   - How you'll store data (encrypted drive, password-protected server, etc.)
   - Who has access to raw data
   - How long you'll retain data
   - De-identification procedures (coding, pseudonyms)
   - Data destruction plan
   - HIPAA/FERPA compliance (if applicable)

7. **Compensation** (if applicable)
   - Will you pay participants? How much?
   - When will they be paid?

8. **Researcher Qualifications**
   - Your training in human subjects research
   - Any prior IRB approvals
   - Your advisor's involvement/oversight

#### Step 4 — Special Populations & Enhanced Protections

**Vulnerable Populations** require additional ethical safeguards:

| Population | Considerations | Protections |
|------------|----------------|------------|
| **Minors (<18 years)** | Cannot legally consent | Parental informed consent + child assent; minimize risk; clear benefits |
| **Prisoners** | At risk of coercion; limited autonomy | Minimize inducements; clear voluntary participation; neutral recruitment |
| **Cognitively Impaired** | Cannot consent independently | Legally authorized representative must consent; participant assent when possible |
| **Pregnant Women** | Potential fetal risk | Minimize risk; clear disclosure; additional safety monitoring |
| **Students/Employees** | Power dynamic with researcher | Use anonymous surveys; ensure no penalty for non-participation |
| **Low Socioeconomic Status** | May feel coerced by compensation | Reasonable compensation; no undue inducement |
| **Non-English Speakers** | Cannot fully consent without translation | Provide translated ICF & verbal explanation by interpreter |

#### Step 5 — Draft Your Informed Consent Form

**Required Components** (per federal regulations):

1. **Purpose & Procedures** (Plain Language)
   - "This research is about understanding [topic]."
   - "If you participate, you will [describe what they do]."

2. **Duration**
   - "The study will take about [X] hours."

3. **Risks & Discomforts**
   - "Risks may include [list all potential harms, even minor ones]."
   - "We will [mitigation strategy]."

4. **Benefits**
   - Honest statement; don't overstate
   - "You may not benefit directly, but this research may help [future application]."

5. **Confidentiality**
   - "We will keep your responses confidential by [method]."
   - "Your data will be stored on [secure location]."
   - Limits to confidentiality (mandatory reporting, etc.)

6. **Right to Withdraw**
   - "You may withdraw at any time without penalty."
   - "Withdrawing will not affect your grade/employment/benefits."

7. **Researcher Contact & IRB Contact**
   - Your name, email, phone
   - Advisor name & contact
   - IRB office contact (usually provided by institution)

8. **Voluntary Participation**
   - "Your participation is voluntary."
   - "You are free to decline or skip questions."

**Readability**
- Use short sentences (15–20 words)
- Avoid jargon
- Test with 6th–8th grade reading level (Flesch-Kincaid)
- Use bullet points and white space

#### Step 6 — Data Security & Compliance

**Security Plan Must Address**

- **Physical Security**: Locked file cabinets for paper records
- **Digital Security**: Encrypted drives, password-protected accounts, two-factor authentication
- **Access Control**: Only you and advisor access raw data
- **De-identification**: Remove names, identifying info; use participant ID codes
- **Data Retention**: How long you'll keep data after study ends
- **Destruction Plan**: How/when you'll securely delete data

**GDPR & Special Considerations** (if recruiting internationally)
- Explicit consent for data processing
- Right to be forgotten (delete personal data on request)
- Data Processing Agreement if using third-party platforms

#### Step 7 — IRB Review Timeline & Process

**Typical Timeline**
- Submission → Initial review: 1–2 weeks
- IRB meeting: Monthly or biweekly
- Full board review: 2–4 weeks
- Revisions & resubmission: 1–2 weeks
- **Total: 1–3 months from submission to approval**

**Your Role**
1. Submit complete protocol + ICF to IRB office
2. Receive initial completeness check
3. Respond to IRB questions/requests for revision
4. Wait for full board meeting
5. Receive approval letter (sometimes with minor/major revisions)
6. Make revisions if requested; resubmit
7. Receive final approval; **do not begin study until approved**

#### Step 8 — Amendments & Ongoing Compliance

**After Approval**

- **Protocol Amendment**: Required if you change methods, add/remove participants, change compensation, recruit a new population, etc.
- **Adverse Event Report**: If a participant is injured or harmed
- **Progress Reports**: Most IRBs require annual renewal; submit brief update
- **Closure Report**: When study ends; submit final report

## IRB Prep Checklist

- [ ] Determine if study requires IRB approval (ask your advisor)
- [ ] Review your institution's IRB guidelines & forms
- [ ] Draft study design with clear participant procedures
- [ ] Identify any vulnerable populations
- [ ] Assess risks; develop mitigation strategies
- [ ] Draft Informed Consent Form at 6th–8th grade reading level
- [ ] Describe confidentiality & data security procedures
- [ ] Prepare researcher qualifications section
- [ ] Collect required institutional forms (protocol, compliance checklist, etc.)
- [ ] Get advisor review before IRB submission
- [ ] Submit complete application to IRB office
- [ ] Respond promptly to IRB requests for clarification
- [ ] **Do not collect data until IRB approval is received**

---

## Ethical Red Flags

🚩 Asking participants for identifying information but claiming confidentiality  
🚩 Minimizing or omitting known risks from the ICF  
🚩 Using coercion or undue inducement for participation  
🚩 Working with minors or vulnerable populations without special protections  
🚩 Inadequate data security measures  
🚩 Collecting data before IRB approval
