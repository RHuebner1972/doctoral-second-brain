# Writing Session Plan

## Purpose
Transform available time into a structured, high-output writing session — with a clear intention, a warm-up, focused deep work blocks, and a deliberate close.

## The Problem With Unstructured Writing Time
Without structure:
- The first 30 minutes are spent re-reading what you already wrote
- The middle time is spent avoiding the hard section
- The last 10 minutes are spent feeling guilty about what didn't get done

With structure:
- Every minute has a job
- The hard section gets attacked first
- The session ends with a completed deliverable

## Session Architecture by Duration

### 60-Minute Session
| Block | Time | Activity |
|-------|------|---------|
| Warm-up | 10 min | Re-read the last paragraph written; set the one-sentence goal |
| Deep work | 40 min | Write — no editing, no re-reading, forward motion only |
| Close | 10 min | Review what was written; set the first sentence of the next session |

### 90-Minute Session
| Block | Time | Activity |
|-------|------|---------|
| Warm-up | 10 min | Re-read last paragraph; set goal; silence notifications |
| Deep work block 1 | 35 min | Draft the hardest section |
| Break | 5 min | Physical movement only — no phone |
| Deep work block 2 | 30 min | Continue or shift to adjacent section |
| Close | 10 min | Review; set up next session; note blockers |

### 2–3 Hour Session
| Block | Time | Activity |
|-------|------|---------|
| Warm-up | 10 min | Re-read; set the session goal (1–3 deliverables) |
| Pomodoro 1 | 25 min writing / 5 min break | Hardest section |
| Pomodoro 2 | 25 min writing / 5 min break | Continue or new section |
| Mid-session check | 5 min | Am I on track? Adjust if needed |
| Pomodoro 3 | 25 min writing / 5 min break | Second section or revision |
| Pomodoro 4 | 25 min writing / 5 min break | Final push |
| Close | 15 min | Review all written; update tracker; set up next session |

## Process

### Step 1 — Gather Inputs
Ask:
1. How much time do you have?
2. What chapter and section are you working on?
3. What is the biggest thing you need to accomplish today?
4. What's been blocking you, if anything?
5. Where did you leave off last session?

### Step 2 — Set the Session Goal
The goal must be:
- **Specific:** "Draft the data analysis justification section" not "work on Chapter 3"
- **Output-based:** Defined by what will be produced, not what will be attempted
- **Achievable:** A 90-minute session can produce 300–600 words of first draft or a revised 2-page section

**Calibration:**
- 25 focused minutes → 150–300 new words (first draft)
- 60 focused minutes → 300–600 new words
- 2 hours → 600–1,200 new words (or 1,000 words of revised/polished text)

### Step 3 — Design the Session
Return a custom session plan based on available time and goal.

### Step 4 — Define Done
Before starting, answer: "How will I know this session was successful?"
> "I will know I'm done when I have a complete draft of the trustworthiness section, even if it's rough."

### Step 5 — Post-Session Debrief Prompt
At the close of the session, the learner should answer:
1. What did I produce?
2. What did I not finish and why?
3. What is the first sentence of my next session?
4. What was my biggest obstacle?

## Session Rules
- **No re-reading and editing at the same time** — pick one mode per block
- **Forward motion first** — get words down; revise in a later session
- **No internet research during writing blocks** — leave [citation needed] placeholders
- **Silence all notifications** before starting the timer

## Output Format
1. **Session goal** (one sentence)
2. **Done condition** (how success is defined)
3. **Timed block schedule** (custom to available time)
4. **Opening warm-up prompt** (the exact question to ask yourself to get started)
5. **Post-session debrief checklist**
