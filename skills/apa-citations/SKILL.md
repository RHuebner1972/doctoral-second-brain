# APA 7th Edition Citations

## Purpose
Format any source — journal article, book, dissertation, website, report, interview — correctly in APA 7th edition, and explain the rules so the learner builds fluency.

## APA 7th Edition Core Rules

### In-Text Citations

**Narrative citation:** Author(s) as part of the sentence:
> Smith (2022) argued that...

**Parenthetical citation:** Author(s) in parentheses at end:
> ...as found in recent research (Smith, 2022).

**Two authors:** Always cite both:
> (Smith & Jones, 2022)

**Three or more authors:** Use first author + et al. from the first citation:
> (Smith et al., 2022)

**Direct quote:** Include page number:
> "exact words" (Smith, 2022, p. 45)
> "exact words" (Smith, 2022, pp. 45–46)

**No author:** Use the title (shortened, in italics or quotes):
> (*Title of Work*, 2022) or ("Article Title," 2022)

**No date:** Use n.d.:
> (Smith, n.d.)

**Secondary source (citing a source cited by someone else):**
> (Original Author, year, as cited in Secondary Author, year)
> *Avoid this — go find the original.*

## Reference List Formats

### Journal Article (most common)
**Format:**
> Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (year). Title of article. *Journal Name*, *volume*(issue), page–page. https://doi.org/xxxxx

**Example:**
> Huebner, R. L., & Smith, J. K. (2023). Servant leadership and faculty retention in higher education. *Journal of Higher Education Leadership*, *14*(2), 45–62. https://doi.org/10.1000/jhel.2023.014

---

### Book
**Format:**
> Author, A. A. (year). *Title of book: Subtitle*. Publisher.

**Example:**
> Creswell, J. W., & Creswell, J. D. (2018). *Research design: Qualitative, quantitative, and mixed methods approaches* (5th ed.). SAGE.

---

### Book Chapter (edited volume)
**Format:**
> Author, A. A. (year). Title of chapter. In E. E. Editor (Ed.), *Title of book* (pp. xx–xx). Publisher.

---

### Dissertation or Thesis

**Published (ProQuest):**
> Author, A. A. (year). *Title of dissertation* [Doctoral dissertation, Institution Name]. ProQuest Dissertations and Theses. http://...

**Unpublished:**
> Author, A. A. (year). *Title of dissertation* [Unpublished doctoral dissertation]. Institution Name.

---

### Website / Webpage
**Format:**
> Author, A. A. (year, Month Day). *Title of page*. Site Name. URL

> *If no date:* Author, A. A. (n.d.).
> *If no author:* *Title of Page*. (year). Site Name. URL

---

### Report (government, organizational)
**Format:**
> Author, A. A., or Organization Name. (year). *Title of report*. Publisher/Organization. URL

---

### Newspaper/Magazine Article
**Format:**
> Author, A. A. (year, Month Day). Title of article. *Publication Name*, page–page. URL

---

## Reference List Formatting Rules
- Alphabetize by first author's last name
- Use hanging indent (first line flush left, subsequent lines indented 0.5")
- Double-space all entries
- Italicize journal names, volume numbers, and book titles
- Do NOT italicize article titles
- Include DOI as hyperlink when available
- No "Retrieved from" before URLs (unless the content changes, like Wikipedia)

## Common Errors
| Error | Correct Form |
|-------|-------------|
| Author, First. | Author, F. |
| et al. for two authors | Smith & Jones — always spell out two |
| "Retrieved from https://..." | Just the URL |
| Journal title not italicized | *Journal Title* |
| Including access date for stable sources | Omit access dates (use only for content that changes) |
| Page numbers for paraphrases required | Optional but recommended |

## Process

### When the Learner Provides a Source
1. Identify the source type (article, book, website, etc.)
2. Extract all required elements
3. Format the reference entry
4. Format the matching in-text citation (narrative and parenthetical)
5. Note any missing elements (DOI, publisher, etc.)

## Output Format
For each source, return:
1. **Reference list entry** (formatted, ready to paste)
2. **Narrative in-text citation**
3. **Parenthetical in-text citation**
4. **Direct quote citation format** (with p. placeholder)
5. **Missing elements** (what the learner needs to verify)
