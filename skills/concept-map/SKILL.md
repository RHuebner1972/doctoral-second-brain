# Concept Map

## Purpose
Generate a structured textual concept map that makes explicit the relationships between the dissertation's key constructs, theories, and research questions — serving as both a thinking tool and a visual model for the dissertation.

## What a Concept Map Does
A concept map:
- Forces clarity about what constructs are central and how they relate
- Reveals logical gaps or disconnections in the argument
- Provides the basis for the conceptual/theoretical framework visual
- Serves as an organizing scaffold for the literature review

## Types of Relationships in a Concept Map

| Relationship Type | Language | Example |
|-----------------|---------|---------|
| **Causal** | leads to, causes, produces | Transformational leadership → psychological safety |
| **Moderating** | is moderated by, depends on | Relationship between X and Y, moderated by Z |
| **Mediating** | mediates, explains the mechanism | X → Z (via M) → Y |
| **Associative** | is associated with, correlates with | Servant leadership ↔ employee engagement |
| **Constitutive** | is a dimension of, is part of | Authenticity → is a dimension of → servant leadership |
| **Sequential** | precedes, follows, leads to | Experience → reflection → conceptual change |
| **Contextual** | is influenced by, occurs within | Leadership behavior [within] organizational culture |

## Process

### Step 1 — Gather the Key Constructs
Ask the learner:
1. What is the central phenomenon you're studying?
2. What are the major constructs or variables in your study?
3. What is the theoretical framework?
4. What are your research questions?

Extract a list of 5–12 key concepts (more than 12 becomes unmanageable).

### Step 2 — Identify the Anchor Concept
One concept is central — it is what the study is fundamentally about. Everything else relates to it.

Example anchor: "faculty retention" in a study of servant leadership and faculty retention.

### Step 3 — Map Relationships
For each concept, ask:
- How does this relate to the anchor concept?
- How does this relate to other concepts in the list?
- Is this relationship causal, associative, moderating, or contextual?

### Step 4 — Build the Textual Map

Use indentation and relationship language to represent the map:

```
[Anchor Concept: Faculty Retention]
│
├── INFLUENCED BY: Organizational Commitment
│       ├── INFLUENCED BY: Servant Leadership
│       │       ├── DIMENSIONS: Empowerment, Humility, Authenticity
│       │       └── GROUNDED IN: Greenleaf's Servant Leadership Theory
│       └── INFLUENCED BY: Psychological Safety
│
├── MODERATED BY: Institutional Context
│       ├── University Type (R1, Teaching, HBCU)
│       └── Departmental Culture
│
└── MEASURED BY: Intent to Leave / Intent to Stay (Turnover Intention Scale)

[Research Questions]
├── RQ1 → Explores relationship: Servant Leadership → Faculty Retention
├── RQ2 → Explores mediating role: Organizational Commitment
└── RQ3 → Explores contextual variation: Institutional Type
```

### Step 5 — Identify Gaps in the Map
Ask:
- Are any relationships implied but not defined?
- Are any constructs present but unconnected?
- Are there constructs in the literature that should be on the map but aren't?
- Does the map reflect the research questions accurately?

### Step 6 — Convert to Visual Description
Describe the map as if giving instructions to a graphic designer:
> "Place [anchor concept] in the center. Draw arrows from [A] to [anchor] labeled 'influences.' Draw a box around [B] and [C] labeled 'moderating variables.' Connect [theoretical framework] to the anchor with a dotted line labeled 'grounds.'"

This description can be given to any diagram tool (Lucidchart, draw.io, Canva, or even PowerPoint).

## Output Format
1. **Construct list** (all key concepts, with brief definitions)
2. **Relationship inventory** (each pair, with relationship type and direction)
3. **Textual concept map** (indented tree with relationship labels)
4. **Gap analysis** (missing relationships or constructs)
5. **Visual description** (instructions for drawing the diagram)
6. **Connection to RQs** (how each RQ maps onto the concept map)
