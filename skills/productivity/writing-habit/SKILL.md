# Writing Habit

## Purpose
Design a sustainable, realistic daily writing practice — one that fits the learner's real life and builds momentum through consistency rather than heroic effort.

## The Core Insight
The doctoral writing crisis is almost never a skill problem. It's a habit problem. Most learners wait until they have a large block of time, the right inspiration, or the right mood. This ensures they almost never write.

The solution: write every day, even badly, even briefly. Consistency beats intensity.

**Research basis:** Boice (1990) found that faculty who wrote in short daily sessions produced more and experienced less block than those who relied on binge writing. The academic writing literature consistently supports daily practice.

## The Minimum Viable Writing Habit

**The 15-Minute Rule:**
Commit to 15 minutes of dissertation writing every single day. Not reading. Not thinking. Writing — producing new text or revising existing text.

Why 15 minutes?
- It's short enough to be undeniable ("I have 15 minutes")
- It's long enough to be productive (150–300 words)
- It maintains the neural pathways that make the next session easier
- Streaks of 15-minute sessions compound rapidly

## Process

### Step 1 — Audit the Current State
Ask the learner:
1. How often are you currently writing (in sessions per week)?
2. When in the day do you feel most mentally sharp?
3. What time slot could you protect for writing — even 15 minutes?
4. What has broken your writing habit in the past?
5. What accountability structures work for you (self-tracking, accountability partner, public commitment)?

### Step 2 — Design the Habit

**Habit anatomy (from Clear, 2018):**
- **Cue:** The trigger that starts the habit (same time, same place, same opening ritual)
- **Routine:** The behavior (15–90 minutes of writing)
- **Reward:** The immediate positive reinforcement (logging the streak, marking the calendar, a ritual close)

**Build the writing habit:**

| Element | Design Question | Example |
|---------|----------------|---------|
| **Cue** | When and where? | 6:00 AM, at the kitchen table, coffee in hand |
| **Routine** | What exactly will you do? | Open the dissertation document; write until the timer goes off |
| **Minimum** | What counts as "done"? | One new sentence on the page |
| **Reward** | How will you mark it? | X on the calendar; a one-sentence log entry |

### Step 3 — Set the Streak

**Tools:**
- Paper calendar with X marks (the Seinfeld method)
- Notion or Obsidian habit tracker
- A simple spreadsheet: date | words written | session length | section

**The goal:** Don't break the chain. When you miss, the rule is: never miss twice in a row.

### Step 4 — Anticipate Obstacles
For each obstacle, create an "if-then" rule:
> "If I don't have my laptop, I will write longhand in my notebook."
> "If I wake up late and miss the morning, I will write for 10 minutes at lunch."
> "If I'm traveling, I will write 100 words on my phone before sleeping."

### Step 5 — Build in Accountability
Options (choose what fits):
- **Writing partner:** Commit to a daily word count check-in with a colleague
- **Online community:** Doctoral writing groups (Academic Writing Month, Shut Up and Write sessions)
- **Coach/Chair:** Share weekly word counts with an accountability relationship
- **Self-tracker:** Daily log with word counts and a brief note on what was written

## Habit Calibration by Phase

| Phase | Daily Minimum | Realistic Target |
|-------|--------------|-----------------|
| Coursework + proposal | 15 min / 150 words | 30–45 min / 300–500 words |
| ABD + active writing | 30 min / 300 words | 60–90 min / 500–1,000 words |
| Data collection | 15 min / 150 words | 30 min / 300 words |
| Analysis + revision | 30 min / 300 words | 45–60 min / 400–600 words |

## Output Format
1. **Current habit audit** (honest assessment of current writing frequency)
2. **Custom habit design** (cue, routine, minimum, reward)
3. **Weekly writing schedule** (specific days and times blocked)
4. **If-then obstacle plans** (top 3 anticipated obstacles)
5. **Accountability structure** (chosen method)
6. **First session plan** — what to write tomorrow morning
