# Goal Setting

## Purpose
Help the learner set clear, tiered doctoral goals — from semester-level vision down to weekly priorities — so that every week's work is in service of a larger, deliberate plan.

## The Doctoral Goal Hierarchy

```
Degree Completion Vision (multi-year)
    └── Semester Goal (3–4 months)
            └── Monthly Milestone (4 weeks)
                    └── Weekly Commitments (3 max)
                            └── Daily Writing Intention (1 task)
```

Each level must be traceable to the one above it. If a weekly task doesn't connect to a monthly milestone, it probably shouldn't be on the list.

## Characteristics of Good Doctoral Goals

**Specific:** Not "work on my dissertation" — "complete a full draft of the trustworthiness section"
**Measurable:** Defined by an output, not an activity
**Achievable:** Calibrated to real available time (not the ideal version of your week)
**Relevant:** Directly advances the dissertation or scholarly identity
**Time-bound:** Has a date — a week, a month, a semester end

## The Goal-Setting Session

### Step 1 — Orientation Check
Ask the learner:
1. What phase of the dissertation are you in?
2. When is your next hard deadline (committee meeting, defense, IRB submission)?
3. How many hours per week realistically can you give to doctoral work right now?
4. What's been going well? What's been stuck?

### Step 2 — Semester Goal
One to two goals that define what a successful semester looks like:
> "By the end of this semester, I will have a committee-approved proposal (Chapters 1–3)."
> "By December 15, I will have submitted my IRB application and completed data collection."

The semester goal should be ambitious but achievable — not a wish, not a certainty.

### Step 3 — Monthly Milestones
Break the semester goal into monthly checkpoints:

| Month | Milestone |
|-------|-----------|
| Month 1 | Chapter 1 complete draft |
| Month 2 | Chapter 2 complete draft |
| Month 3 | Chapter 3 complete draft + proposal submission |

### Step 4 — Weekly Commitments
From the monthly milestone, identify this week's 3 commitments:
- **No more than 3** — overcommitting is the most common failure mode
- Each must have a **done condition** ("draft complete" not "work on it")
- At least 1 must involve **putting new words on a page**

### Step 5 — Today's Intention
One task, right now, today:
> "The one thing I will do for my dissertation today is ___."

This is the entry point. Not a session plan. Just the next thing.

## Goal Review Cadence
| Frequency | Review Type |
|-----------|------------|
| Daily | Identify today's one intention |
| Weekly | Review commitments; set next 3 |
| Monthly | Assess milestone progress; adjust if needed |
| Semester | Evaluate semester goal; celebrate or diagnose |

## When Goals Are Consistently Not Met

If 2+ weeks of commitments are unmet, this is a signal — not a character flaw. Investigate:
1. **Are the goals too ambitious?** → Halve them. Seriously.
2. **Is the time not protected?** → Schedule writing like a meeting
3. **Is something blocking the work itself?** → Invoke `procrastination-audit`
4. **Is motivation depleted?** → Invoke `motivation-reset`
5. **Is the plan wrong?** → Revisit `dissertation-timeline`

## Output Format
1. **Semester goal** (1–2 sentences)
2. **Monthly milestones** (one per month through semester end)
3. **This week's 3 commitments** (with done conditions)
4. **Today's intention** (one task)
5. **Risk check** — what is most likely to derail this plan, and what's the mitigation?
